ARCHMAGOS MORTIVAR OF MARS

The Many-Formed  |  Taghmata Mortivar  |  Traitor Dark Mechanicum

Few names in the Red Vaults of Olympus Mons provoke as much unease as that of Archmagos Mortivar, once a doctrinal savant of the Helios Synod and now an increasingly unsettling presence within the Mechanicum's war councils. Mortivar was forged on Mars in the crucible of its most radical research orders, rising to prominence through a series of forbidden treatises on continuity of consciousness — papers that skirted dangerously close to the divide between sanctioned soul-capture technologies and outright heretekal digitisation of the mind.

Where others argued that identity was indivisible, Mortivar insisted the self could be copied, stored, and reborn. For this he earned censure, then grudging respect, then — once his theories proved true — fear.

I

The Many Deaths of Mortivar

Mortivar is known to have died six times.

Each time, the ruin of his former vessel is simply the prelude to the awakening of another one: a vat-grown form or steel-forged chassis prepared in advance, into which one of his countless replicated encephalic imprints is poured. To Mortivar, death is merely a discontinuity in the data-flow. To his allies and enemies, it is proof that the Archmagos cannot be ended by conventional means.

He sometimes speaks of himself in plural.

II

The Taghmata Mortivar

Mortivar's personal war-host is as unsettling as its master. His Taghma, a levy drawn from forge-world auxilia, is fiercely loyal — conditioned through hypno-scripts and bound by oaths of mutual augmentation. They march behind him as he glides across the battlefield upon his Abeyant, surrounded by a court of Myrmidon Secutors who have sworn themselves to his doctrine of multiplicity.

To witness Mortivar in war is to see a calculating intelligence directing fire with machine-perfect grace while simultaneously running subsidiary thought-thread clusters across the wider front. Voices issue from vox-grilles on his body and his Myrmidons in answering chorus, the whole unit behaving as a single destructive algorithm.

III

Allegiances and Alliances

Mortivar maintains unusual — some say uncomfortably close — ties with House Vextrix and the Legio Mortis. The bond is older than the Heresy, born from secretive joint research into psi-shunted command relays and Titan cortex backflow monitoring. Both groups appreciate Mortivar's clarity of purpose and the cold utility of a commander who cannot truly die.

Some whisper that Mortivar's memory-echoes extend far beyond his own bodies, that prototypes of his replication lattice have been gifted to the technosavants of the Death's Heads to be tested within Titan manifold minds. Whether this is truth or slander, neither Vextrix nor Mortis has denied it.

IV

The Coming Rupture

With the galaxy at war, Mortivar views the Heresy not as a tragedy but as a crucible — a chance to refine the machine-future he believes the Emperor was too timid to embrace. The division of the Mechanicum offers him freedom: to perfect his doctrine, to expand his network of replicated selves, and to ensure that when the age of flesh ends, Mortivar will endure in forms uncountable.

For the Archmagos, immortality is not merely a goal.
It is an inevitability — already achieved in data, waiting only to be realised in iron.
