RADIANCES TOTALUS IV — THE CUSP OF THE EYE

Theatre Record  |  Eye of Terror Fringe  |  Taghmata Mortivar

Radiances Totalus IV sits at the very fringe of the Eye of Terror — a world ancient enough to predate Imperial cartography, its ruins layered over ruins, its substrate saturated with aeons of warp-bleed. To most commanders this makes it a cursed posting, a theatre to be endured. To Mortivar, it makes it a library.

I

The Manifold of the Dread March Eternal

The primary objective — the wreck of the Legio Mortis Imperator-class Titan Dread March Eternal — was not chosen by high command. Mortivar selected it. An Imperator's manifold is the most complex machine-mind ever constructed: a lattice of princeps memory-imprints, machine-spirit sub-cortices, and accumulated battle-consciousness spanning centuries of active service. When such a Titan falls, that lattice does not simply cease. It persists, degrading, in whatever portions of the reactor and command deck survive.

The synaptic relics extracted from the Dread March Eternal were fragments of exactly that lattice. To Mortivar they were not trophies. They were data — the richest concentration of non-biological consciousness architecture he had ever recovered. He secured the wreck under his sigil within a single solar hour and began extraction immediately. The loyalists were an obstacle. The relics were the reason.

His standing with Legio Mortis improved considerably. He did not mention that the improvement was incidental.

II

What the Eye Remembers

The exo-planar encryption cores buried beneath Byzant Aeturnis were a secondary target — and their loss stings more than Mortivar admits. Technology capable of encrypting data transmissions against warp interference would allow him to maintain the integrity of memory-imprints across interstellar distances without physical transport. His network of replicated selves is presently constrained by the unreliability of warp-transit for data payloads. Those cores would have removed the constraint. They remain buried.

The third engagement — the extraction from Ghul-Kharas through World Eater betrayal and Knightly treachery — Mortivar had modelled as a probable outcome before landing on the planet. He had prepared accordingly. The relic-tech was evacuated. His enemies destroyed one another. The campaign closed as a net positive in his ledger, which is the only ledger he keeps.

The Eye of Terror is not a wound in the galaxy.
It is a record of everything that was lost.
Mortivar intends to read it.
