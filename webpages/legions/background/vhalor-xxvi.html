VHALOR XXVI — THE IRON RECKONING

Theatre Record  |  Hive Karthax  |  Taghmata Mortivar

Vhalor XXVI is not a world of consequence. Its single hive, Karthax, exists only to service the warp corridor that runs through its system — a minor artery in the vast circulatory logic of Imperial supply.

Then the Iron Reckoning fell from the sky and changed the calculus entirely.

I

Precious Cargo

To the wider war, the Iron Reckoning represents encrypted fleet-routing archives and Legion deployment logs — intelligence worth fighting over. To Mortivar, it represents something more specific. In the months before the Heresy broke, IVth Legion operatives raided Mechanicum waystation-archives along this corridor and took, among other data, fragmented copies of Mortivar's own consciousness-replication research. That research is aboard the ship. He intends to recover it.

II

The Calculus of Advance

Mortivar's Autokratorii Regiment and Legio Cybernetica Cohort are not siege forces. They will not be first through the walls — they will consolidate what others break open. The Warhound of Legio Mortis marches in support, its manifold carrying a cortex-monitoring relay of Mortivar's own design. House Vextrix's Calculus of Ruin holds the vanguard. The Taghmata advances to the perimeter. The Hive walls must be secured by Mortivar's Taghmata to ensure subsequent data recovery.

The Iron Warriors stole from us.
That which was taken is ours.
Data does not forget where it belongs.
