THE SLAUGHTER AT LENTON

30th January 2026  |  Warhammer World, Nottingham  |  Early Heresy



In the ash-choked aftermath of the Dropsite Massacre, a shattered Chapter of the Salamanders Legion fell back upon the hive world of Lenton, seeking sanctuary amongst its manufactoria-spires and forge-warrens. Scarred veterans regrouped beneath the hive's iron skyline, joined by a pair of Legio Defensor Reaver Titans whose god-engines stood as grim sentinels against the coming storm.

The storm came swiftly.

BATTLE 01

Lenton Encirclement

vs. Salamanders & Legio Defensor DECISIVE VICTORY

Objective: Annihilate the Loyalist forces in the city

Dispatched from Mars under writs sealed in binharic and blasphemy, Archmagos Mortivar led his Dark Mechanicum taghmata into the Lenton exclusion zone. Alongside him marched the war engines of House Vextrix, their Knight Atropos striding at the fore, and the deathly silhouettes of a Legio Mortis Warhound Titan, whose arrival darkened auspex and omen alike. Methodically, mercilessly, the loyalists were encircled.

The battle opened in brutal fashion on the eastern approaches. A pair of Knight Atrapos, supported by a talon of Armigers, drove straight into one of the advancing Defensor Reavers. Arcane gravitic weaponry and las-cutters tore into the Titan's carapace at point-blank range. Before the god-engine could bring its full armament to bear, it was overwhelmed — its legs crippled, its reactor breached, and its carcass brought crashing down amidst the ruins, never having a chance to influence the wider battle.

On the right flank, Mortivar himself descended into the inferno. Surrounded by his Myrmidon bodyguard and borne aloft by his arcane war-platform, the Archmagos led a direct assault against a Salamanders Mastodon grinding its way through the hive streets. Volleys of esoteric fire and mechadendrite assaults tore the ancient assault carrier apart, reducing it to a burning wreck. Moments later, Mortivar and his entire command retinue were annihilated under the combined fury of a talon of Saturnine-pattern Dreadnoughts. The Archmagos' physical form was utterly destroyed — his first recorded death in combat — even as his noospheric echoes vanished into the datastream.

Elsewhere, the Salamanders fought with the stubborn fury for which they were renowned. On the right flank, they punched a bloody breach through Mortivar's taghmata, driving back tech-thrall covenants and wrecking multiple constructs in close quarters. For a moment, escape seemed possible.

But then the loyalist right flank collapsed.

With the loyalists' formations unraveling, the combined forces of House Vextrix and the Dark Mechanicum surged forward. Tech-Thralls, Harpax swarmers, and corrupted automata flooded the ruins in overwhelming numbers. Outmatched and outflanked, the Salamanders were dragged down by sheer mass — chainblades clogged with bodies even as the machines kept coming.

The final act played out beneath the shadow of a dying god. The surviving Defensor Reaver stood fast, pouring fire into the encroaching traitor host to buy time for what few Salamanders still lived. One by one, loyalist units disengaged and fled into the depths of Lenton's lower hive.

The Reaver did not follow.

Caught in the open, it was methodically destroyed by massed fire from a Mortis Warhound Titan, its turbo-laser supported by the punishing volleys of Mortivar's Karacnos tanks. When the smoke cleared, only wreckage remained — and silence.

Of the Salamanders who had sought refuge on Lenton, only a handful escaped the encirclement. The rest were consumed by steel, fire, and cold Martian logic. Lenton fell not with a single killing blow, but beneath the weight of inevitability — and the Dark Mechanicum recorded the battle as a total material success.

// CAMPAIGN OUTCOME //
1 DECISIVE VICTORY

Lenton fell utterly. The Salamanders' Chapter was destroyed, both Legio Defensor Reavers were claimed as scrap, and the hive world was added to the Dark Mechanicum's growing territorial ledger. The Archmagos' first combat death was noted in the records — and deemed an acceptable data-point.
